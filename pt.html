Question 1:

Answer: The overall purpose of this was to be able to have time management, be able to make codes
by ourselves due to our ideas for the app, to improve our decision making and debugging skills,
and have fun editing our app visually. Doing this gives us experience for web development.
My app is a social media app called PickYou. My app is not like Instagram or Twitter where all kinds of
people and categories are in one place. In my app you have to be older than 14 and live in the USA
(Input). If you met these requirements you would pick your category Art & fashion or Music & Dance
and it will take you to each separate social media regarding which interest you pick (output).
If, younger than 14, live outside of the USA, or both you will be taken to a page denying you
to use the app and explaining why (output).


Question 2:

Answer:Two different classmates gave me feedback, Tesheni, and Sabbine.
They said that I had very good ideas for my app and they liked how it looked visually but coding wise
the app wasn't really working. Some buttons weren't working. Sometimes some buttons worked and
sometimes they didn't. There was a lot to debug and they specified that perfectly. Their ideas were
to focus more on debugging the code so that whoever was using the app could work through it perfectly.
So that's what I did. I debugged my code and my code started working. I was able to travel through all
the screens I meant to travel to depending on what button I chose. This improved my app because there
was no more crashing. However, I believe a "Back to home page" button is needed. The feedback helps you
app a lot because you get different point of views from different people and if they give you the same point
of view it means that it's a really good and true point they're making.
